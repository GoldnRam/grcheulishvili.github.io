# Technological Sovereignty

True national security cannot rely on imported "Black Boxes." 

In the digital domain, a nation that relies entirely on foreign security vendors, external cloud infrastructure, and closed-source defense mechanisms effectively outsources its sovereignty. When the supply chain is cut, or the vendor is compromised, the capability evaporates.

**IERI.SH** is a research initiative focused on reducing this dependency. 

### The Objective
To elevate the regional engineering baseline from "Consumer of Security" to "Architect of Security." We do not just configure tools; we build them.

### 1. Capability over Configuration
Knowing how to configure an EDR is an administrative skill. Knowing how to bypass it—and consequently, how to build a better one—is an engineering skill. We prioritize the latter.

### 2. Infrastructure Independence
A sovereign defense requires sovereign infrastructure. We research methods to deploy resilient, self-hosted telemetry and command structures that do not rely on third-party benevolence.

### 3. Deep Technical Autonomy
We reject high-level abstractions in favor of fundamental understanding. Whether it is kernel-level interactions, memory management, or network protocols, we aim to understand the system at the level where the laws of computing apply, not just the user manual.
